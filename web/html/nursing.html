This section contains specific considerations related to this topic. Standard assessment and documentation practices should still be followed.
  • 1. Blood Glucose and Subcutaneous Insulin Record
    • An accurate blood glucose and subcutaneous insulin record is important in helping the patient achieve their blood glucose targets.
    • The physician is to review and titrate insulin every 1 to 3 days to achieve targets.
    • For each meal, at bedtime and any additional times as specified by the physician, it is important to document the following:
      • i. Time the capillary blood glucose was taken
      • ii. The glucometer result of the capillary blood glucose
      • iii. The type (basal, bolus, correction or other), brand and amount of insulin given
      • iv. The time and site the insulin was administered
